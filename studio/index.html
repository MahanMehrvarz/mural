MURAL Studio

Offline SVG processor — prepare artwork anywhere, export to the device.

01
Upload SVG

Choose the artwork file you want to draw. SVG line art works best — black strokes on a white background. Filled shapes will be traced as outlines.

Drop an SVG here, or click to browse
02
Canvas Size

Set how large the drawing will be on the wall, in millimeters. A good starting point is 60 % of your pin-to-pin distance for width — this leaves enough margin on both sides for the belt geometry to stay accurate.

700 × auto mm Upload an SVG to preview it here

03
Position & Zoom

Shift or scale the artwork within the fixed canvas. The canvas dimensions stay the same — only where the artwork sits inside them changes. Use this to centre the subject or zoom in to fill the space. Artwork outside the canvas boundary will be clipped.

04
Processing Options

Choose how to convert the SVG into plotter paths, then tune the output. You can re-process with different settings as many times as you like before exporting.

Render mode

Follows SVG paths directly. Fast and clean. Best for line art.

Rasterises first, then re-traces. Preserves varying stroke widths but takes longer.

Infill density 0

0 = outlines only. Higher values add horizontal fill lines inside closed shapes.

Despeckle 2

Removes small noise blobs that appear during rasterisation.

Merges overlapping filled areas so the pen doesn't retrace the same region twice.

05
Process & Export

Click Process SVG to convert your artwork into plotter commands. Processing runs in your browser — it may take 10–60 seconds for complex files. Review the path preview before exporting.
